"Unlock Your Vitality: Discover Nutrient-Rich Foods for a Healthier You!"

Vitamin A, also known as retinol, plays a crucial role in maintaining healthy vision, skin, and immune function. It's often associated with carrots and other orange foods.

Vitamin B1, also known as thiamine, plays a crucial role in the conversion of glucose into energy. It's majorly found in whole grains, fortified cereals and nuts.

Vitamin B2, also known as riboflavin, which is essential for various bodily functions. It's highly found in green leafy vegetables, dairy products and eggs.

Vitamin B3, also known as niacin, plays a crucial role for DNA repair and synthesis. It's majorly found in Green leafy vegetables ,Mushrooms, Avocado.

Vitamin B5, or pantothenic acid, is vital for energy metabolism and is involved in the synthesis of fatty acids and cholesterol. It's found in meat and vegetables.

Vitamin B6, or pyridoxine, is essential for brain development. It's involved in the metabolism of amino acids. Foods like bananas, poultry are good sources.

Vitamin B7, commonly referred to as biotin, is an essential water-soluble vitamin. It is particularly important for the metabolism of fats and amino acids.

Vitamin B12, or cobalamin, is critical for formation of red blood cells. It's mainly found in meat and dairy. Vitamin B12 deficiency can lead to neurological issues.

Vitamin C, or ascorbic acid, is well-known for its immune-boosting properties. It's found in citrus fruits and plays a vital role in collagen production.

Vitamin D, often called the "sunshine vitamin," is crucial for immune function. Exposal to sunlight helps the body produce vitamin D, and also found in some supplements.

Vitamin E is a powerful antioxidant that helps protect cells. It's often associated with nuts and vegetable oils and contributes to skin health and immune support.
